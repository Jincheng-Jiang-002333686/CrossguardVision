## 1. Comparison experiment — direction decided

- The comparison is now **all-Ultralytics YOLO-seg**. No external frameworks
  (MMDetection / Detectron2 were dropped — don't fit the Windows setup; YOLACT unmaintained).
- **Rejected candidates and why:** Faster R-CNN, SSD, and VGG16 are box-only (or a
  backbone, in VGG16's case — it is literally SSD's backbone) → they cannot produce the
  **crosswalk mask** the downstream spatial-reasoning stage depends on. Mask R-CNN /
  YOLACT were the correct segmentation analogs but added cross-framework friction
  (COCO conversion, pycocotools-vs-Ultralytics mAP parity) for little gain.
- Going all-Ultralytics removes that friction: same data format, one training command
  (`--model …`), and **one identical eval** so all numbers are directly comparable
  (no COCO/pycocotools conversion needed).

## 2. Models under test (5 total) — 2 versions × scales

Design: **YOLO11-seg** {n, s, m} + **YOLO26-seg** {n, s}. Lets us read two axes —
**scale** (n→s→m, the accuracy-vs-speed curve for the Pi) and **version**
(YOLO11 vs YOLO26 at matched n and s scales). YOLO26 is NMS-free + DFL-removed, an
edge/CPU-oriented design (Ultralytics report up to ~43% faster CPU inference), so it is
the most interesting candidate for Raspberry Pi deployment.
